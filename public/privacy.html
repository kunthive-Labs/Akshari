Last updated: July 19, 2026

1. Who controls your data

Kunthive Labs operates akshari.kunthive.in and is the data controller for this deployment. If you are using a self-hosted instance run by someone else, that operator is the controller instead - see section 12.

2. What we process

Akshari has no accounts, no sign-in, and no forms that collect personal details. What it processes is limited to:

  • Search and filter input - the text and tags you use to browse the catalog, handled entirely in your browser against a static export of the catalog. It is not sent to, or stored on, our servers.
  • Technical request metadata - standard web server/CDN logs (IP address, user agent, requested path, timestamp, status code) generated automatically when your browser loads the site.
  • Local device storage - your appearance (light/dark) preference and your cookie-consent choice, stored only in your browser. See section 4.
  • Font specimen requests - when a specimen loads, your browser requests that font file directly from Google's font-serving infrastructure. See section 5.

4. Cookies & local storage

Akshari does not use advertising or tracking cookies, and does not run analytics. The cookie banner shown on first visit lets you choose what local storage is allowed:

CategoryWhat it storesConsent required?
NecessaryYour consent choice itself (so we don't ask again every visit)No - required to operate the banner
PreferencesLight/dark appearance, set only if you accept this categoryYes

You can accept, reject, or change these choices at any time from "Cookie settings" in the site footer. Rejecting Preferences means your theme choice applies only to your current visit and is not remembered on your next one.

5. Third parties

Akshari is a Google Fonts browser, so loading it necessarily loads webfont CSS and font files from fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com. Those requests go directly from your browser to Google's servers - they are not proxied by us. Google states that this delivery does not use cookies and is not used to build advertising profiles, though the requests still reach Google's infrastructure and may be logged there for operational purposes. See Google's privacy policy for how Google handles that traffic.

Our hosting/CDN provider processes the request logs described in section 2 on our behalf, under its own data-processing terms.

6. International transfers

Font requests to Google and our hosting/CDN provider may be handled on infrastructure outside your country, including outside the EEA. Where that happens, the provider's standard safeguards (such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses) apply.

7. Retention

Operational logs are retained only as long as needed to diagnose reliability and security issues, then deleted or aggregated. Search and filter input is never transmitted to us, so we retain none of it. Local storage (theme, consent record) stays on your device until you clear it or withdraw consent.

8. Your rights

Under GDPR you can ask us to:

  • confirm what data we hold about you and get a copy (access);
  • correct inaccurate data (rectification);
  • delete data we hold (erasure), where applicable;
  • restrict or object to processing based on legitimate interest;
  • receive data you provided in a portable format (portability);
  • withdraw consent at any time, without affecting past lawful processing.

Because Akshari collects so little personal data by design, most requests will simply confirm that no identifiable record exists beyond short-lived operational logs. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

9. Security

The service is served over HTTPS, applies standard security response headers, and rate-limits requests to reduce abuse. No system is perfectly secure; report suspected vulnerabilities through the channel in section 12.

10. Children

Akshari is a general-audience design tool and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

11. Changes

We may update this notice as the service changes. Material changes will update the date at the top of this page; continued use after a change means you accept the current version.

12. Contact & self-hosting

For privacy requests or questions, contact the maintainer through the project repository. Self-hosted operators control their own logs, backups, deployment configuration, and data retention, and should adapt this notice to describe their own deployment before publishing it.
